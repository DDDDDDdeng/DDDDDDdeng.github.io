About me

Hello! I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Geosciences at Virginia Tech, working under the supervision of Dr. Ying Zhou.

My research focuses on Seismology. I am passionate about constraining Core–Mantle Boundary (CMB) and deeper earth structures using differential seismic phases.

Prior to joining VT, I received my Bachelor's degree in Geophysics from Yangtze University and my Master's degree in Geophysics from the Institute of Geophysics, China Earthquake Administration.

Research

Research Interests

  • Core–Mantle Boundary (CMB): Constraining seismic heterogeneities and topography in the CMB region.
  • Mantle Transition Zone: Investigating the structural and velocity anomalies in the mantle.
  • Global Seismology & Tomography: Utilizing traveltimes and finite-frequency techniques to resolve deep earth structures.

Recent Research Activities

  • Downloaded and processed seismic data from 2020 to 2025.
  • Obtained ScS-S differential travel time measurements.
  • Performed simple inversions on the data.

Research Plans

  • Increase Seismic Data Coverage: Incorporate data from additional earthquakes and seismic stations.
    Rationale: Expanding the dataset will fill existing spatial gaps in the current ray-path map and ensure more robust inversion results.
  • Assess global tomographic models in the lowermost mantle: Evaluate existing global seismic velocity models (e.g., S40RTS, S362ANI) by comparing predicted and observed differential traveltimes.
    Rationale: Helps identify model-dependent biases and provides a baseline for interpreting new inversion results.
  • Perform Finite-Frequency Tomography: Apply finite-frequency tomographic techniques to the expanded dataset.
    Rationale: To resolve a higher-resolution velocity structure of the Core-Mantle Boundary (CMB).
